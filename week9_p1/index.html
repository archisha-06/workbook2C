Blink

Project: Simple LED Light Circuit

For Week 9, we got into Web Serial API stuff—basically a way to make your browser talk to your Arduino through a USB port. We had to use Chrome (or anything Chromium-based), plug in a library using a CDN, and do a bit of setup in both Arduino and p5. Not too painful, once everything decided to cooperate. The main idea was to use a potentiometer to control the brightness of a p5 sketch. The Arduino sends values, and the sketch listens in using this dataIn variable. So as you turn the knob, the background of the canvas gets lighter or darker. Simple, but kind of satisfying to see physical movement reflected on-screen. We also added code to send data back from the browser—like mouse positions—so the two sides could actually communicate. Sort of like: “Hey Arduino, here’s where my mouse is,” and Arduino’s like, “Cool, here’s how bright I am.” You have to click the canvas to choose the port, and if you forget to close the Arduino Serial Monitor, nothing works and everything complains. But once it's up and running, it’s smooth. The blinking LED helps to confirm that things are alive.
