Active Theory
The website by Active Theory has been a huge source of inspiration for mine—especially in how it balances interactivity with aesthetic precision. I love how their site feels immersive without being overwhelming; the animations are smooth, purposeful, and always support the content rather than distract from it. That’s something I’ve tried to apply to my own site—creating subtle motion, like floating links and animated backgrounds, to bring the page to life. I’m also really drawn to their use of dark themes with glowing, neon-like elements, which aligns with the cyberpunk-tech style I’ve been developing. The site feels more like an experience than just a portfolio, and that’s the direction I want to head in: something interactive, visually cohesive, and just a bit futuristic. Website
Rain Room
Rain Room by Random International really stuck with me. There’s something so beautiful about the way it gives people this quiet control over a natural force, like you’re parting the rain just by existing. The motion sensors respond to where you walk, so it feels like the environment is listening to you in real time. It’s kind of surreal, but also calming in a way. That idea of responsive space definitely inspired parts of my own work. Some of my falling number backgrounds were actually influenced by it—especially the way the rain in Rain Room creates this constant digital flow, but pauses the moment you interact with it. I loved that contrast and wanted to bring a bit of that into my site. It reminded me that tech doesn’t always have to scream for attention—it can also whisper, react, and create a space that feels alive with you in it.. Website
